Assess, Decide, Do

Despite the similarity in name, Assess Decide Do is not connected in any way with our own Add Share Do.  

You might like to learn about their "life management framework" (yes, it's not just an app, but they do have an app and a book) and it might be the framework you've been looking for.  We don't know, but from what we've learned about Assess Decide Do it looks you can apply the methodology using our own ASD.

The steps appear to be:

  • "Assess - overload your internal system with information: add tasks, ideas, events or projects."
  • "Decide - 'sign the contract' to actually do that task or project."
  • "Do - you see only what you decided earlier. Not extra clutter, not floating tasks or events, only what you decide you really want to do."

These can be mapped to a couple of tags in Add Share Do.  For example, perhaps you have a tag called #assess or #backlog or #maybe.  That's what you tag all ideas with.  In fact, because Add Share Do automatically tags bookmarks and tasks by email with #viaBookmarklet and #viaEmail you could create a Group called "Assess" that contains the tag you pick and #viaEmail and #viaEmail. (I call my group "Inbox", BTW)

The Do part is another tag, and the natural name would be #do.

Assessing then becomes a process you go through of looking down you #access tag or group, and changing the tag to #do.

When you want to get down to it, just focus in on #do and ... do stuff.

That's another easy way to use Add Share Do.  If you've not signed up (and why not?), try it today for free, no credit card needed, no limits on features.
