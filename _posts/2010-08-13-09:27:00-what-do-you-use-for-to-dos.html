What do you use for to dos?

I asked the fine people of the Brighton New Media mailing list the following: What are people using for shared to do lists?  I don't mean issue trackers or story tools, I just mean you have a bunch of things to do that you want to share with a couple of people in a light-ish way.

Plenty of answers came back, and I've included some of the comments I received (thank you for the comments!):

  • Remember the milk (from 3 people, saying "I use Remember the Milk for more complex organisation of tasks" and "If really light to-do lists are all you want")
  • A spreadsheet in Google Docs (from 2 people, "Although there are apps out there like Remember the Milk, Ta Da & Toodledoo etc, it sounds like you just want something really basic so I'd recommend uploading a Google Doc & giving admin rights to whoever you're working with")
  • Agilezen
  • Tadalist ("supposed to be very easy in use")
  • Google bookmarks
  • Gub ("love Gubb for its simplicity")
  • Teux Deux
  • Things ("If you and your team are on Mac, iPad or iPhone")
  • Toodledo ("If you are into GTD")
  • Shareflow
  • Base Camp ("The To-do facility in particular is very handy as it you can attribute tasks to specific bods, add due dates and comments, and include clients in certain lists and exclude them from others")
  • Paprika ("quite like the plain text approach to creating lists")
  • PlanHQ ("which is a kind of a business planning tool, that combines goals, and tasks, as to-do lists for multiple people")
  • Todobedobedo ("a simple and beautiful shared to do list tool")
  • Thinger (this one might not have been mentioned)

Of course there are many more, but interesting to see what apps are being suggested or recommended.
